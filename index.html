Executive Summary - January - February 2026

From GenAI experiments to agentic integration and workforce redesign

Over the past year, the story has moved from isolated generative AI (GenAI) pilots to operational integration. AI is being embedded into workflows, roles are being redesigned around new task splits, and governance is becoming more concrete. This briefing summarises ten recurring shifts, highlights published examples, and gives leaders questions they can use immediately.

Implementation: the 10 / 20 / 70 rule (rule of thumb)

In most deployments, the model is not the hard part. Outcomes depend mainly on redesigning work, clarifying decision rights, and changing habits. Use this as a rule of thumb when planning effort and ownership.

This is a heuristic, not a law. If you cite it publicly, label it as a rule of thumb and link to your supporting source.

Trust gap

Trust gap (why shadow AI appears): Many employees use AI tools, but fewer trust them enough to rely on them consistently. That gap pushes use into informal, ungoverned channels unless safe use is easy: approved tools, clear do's and don'ts, and peer learning built into day-to-day work.

Governance 2026

The UK remains principles-based, but ICO, FCA, and Ofcom are tightening sector-specific expectations. Employment law and regulatory expectations are evolving. Employers should watch consultation proposals and regulator guidance on monitoring, privacy, and high-impact automated decisions.
